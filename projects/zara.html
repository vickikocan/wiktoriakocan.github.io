ZARA Case Study

In 2020, everyone was forced to become more reliant on online shopping. It went from being a pleasant convenience, to a modern necessity. Because of this, I had noticed more and more people criticising the ZARA website and app. It had become a common joke to mention their negative experience with it, and I decided I wanted to find out exactly why people were feeling this way, and what the ZARA customers really wanted.

Positive comments mentioned the unique layout, how it felt like looking through a magazine. However, the most common negatives mentioned the confusing layout, the long menu, and the images being too big.

Ease of use

Next, I conducted usability testing. The most frequent negative comments were:

  1. The menu is too long, therefore overwhelming
  2. The layout is confusing. Columns were inconsistent
  3. Images are too large and sometimes are not focused on the product itself

The most frequent positive comments were:

  1. Unique, editorial aesthetic
  2. It is easy to navigate
  3. It is responsive

Then, I created user personas:

  • Zoe Pattinson

    Zoe is a 22 year-old business student who lives in Birmingham, UK. Her schedule is usually quite busy, as she spends her days studying, attending lectures, working at her part-time job, and socialising.

    This means she doesn’t have a lot of time to go shopping; but she loves fashion and knows all the latest trends.

    Zoe will browse retailers’ websites and apps in-between all of her daily tasks, not necessarily when she needs to purchase something.

    If she doesn’t enjoy scrolling through a particular app or website, she simply won’t use it, even if she likes the products.

  • Marie Kim

    Marie is a 43 year-old estate agent and mum of two teenagers, who happily aid her in staying on trend.

    She loves to look and feel youthful, and fashion helps her to achieve that. It is also important for her to look and feel good in front of clients.

    Already having an established and stable career means that Marie doesn’t have to worry too much about the cost of things, but she still likes to be practical and get her money’s worth.

    This also means that she likes things to be of good quality, so they will last her as long as possible.

  • Lloyd Dailey

    Lloyd is a 27 year-old living in London, UK. He has a large group of friends and coworkers, and often browses shops around London, rarely using apps or ordering online, as he is rarely home to receive the delivery. This leaves him hoping that, when he does shop online, it will be a smooth and easy process.

    Lloyd is also popular on social media, such as Instagram, so enjoys putting together an impressive outfit to post online.

    Being confident and with a quickly improving career, Lloyd buys first then checks the prices. It is important that his clothes are impressive to his friends, and cares little about how long the items will last; he shops frequently and will quickly replace something worn out.
